Welcome! I recently received my PhD in Biostatistics from Harvard University under the guidance of my wonderful advisors, Brent Coull and Jeffrey Miller. My research into the effects of air pollution on human health, as well as my involvement with the Harvard Center for Climate, Health, and the Global Environment (C-CHANGE), have shaped my desire to work on problems in climate and sustainability for the next chapter of my career.

Before beginning graduate school, I was an Associate Product Manager at Applied Predictive Technologies (since acquired by Mastercard) where I worked with software engineers and consultants to help companies design and analyze business experiments. I completed my undergraduate studies in Statistics at Harvard University with a minor in Chemistry. For my undergraduate thesis, I collaborated with the Melton Lab to perform a screening experiment to identify potential therapeutics for type-I diabetes.

In the pre-Covid era, during my free time I liked to go to concerts (Boston Pops, Harvard Pops, anything John Williams) and play sports (tennis, kickball, and soccer).
